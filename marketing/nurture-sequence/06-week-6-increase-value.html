If you're 12 months out from selling, this email might be the highest-ROI thing in your inbox this year. Five value levers I'd pull, ranked by impact:

1️⃣ Reduce owner dependence (worth +0.5–1.5× multiple)

Document SOPs. Cross-train staff. Take a 2-week vacation and prove the business runs without you. This is the single biggest lever — and it requires zero capital, just calendar time.

2️⃣ Diversify customer concentration (+0.3–0.8×)

If your top customer is >25% of revenue, the buyer will discount. If it's >40%, they'll walk. In the 12 months before listing, intentionally over-invest in winning a few mid-sized accounts to bring concentration down.

3️⃣ Clean up the books (+0.2–0.5×)

Switch to accrual accounting if you're on cash. Get three years of reviewed (or audited) financials. Document add-backs with invoices. Buyers pay a premium for clean defensible numbers — and pay nothing for "trust me, the books look messy but the business is solid."

4️⃣ Build recurring revenue (+0.5–2.0× in some industries)

Even small shifts matter. A trades business adding maintenance contracts. An e-commerce store adding subscriptions. A consultancy adding monthly retainers. If you can show a growing recurring-revenue line in the trailing 12 months, the multiple expands meaningfully.

5️⃣ Show 12 strong months of growth (+0.3–0.8×)

Buyers underwrite trailing twelve months. If your TTM is up 15% YoY going into a sale, the multiple stretches. If it's flat or declining, no amount of "but the next 12 will be better" gets you a premium. Time the listing to your strongest TTM.

A typical $500K SDE service business that pulls all five levers can move from a 2.8× sale to a 4.5× sale. That's $850K in extra proceeds for 12 months of intentional preparation.

I tell every owner the same thing: a year of preparation before you list is worth more than five years of growth after.
