Yes, I'm a broker. Yes, I'll tell you when not to use one. Here's the actual decision framework:

When you DON'T need a broker

  • Business value under ~$250K. The math doesn't work for the broker or you. List on BizBuySell, hire a transaction attorney for $3–5K, do it yourself.
  • You already have a known buyer. A key employee, a competitor, a family member. You don't need a marketing process — you need a closing process. Skip the broker fee, hire an M&A attorney.
  • You're in a niche where buyer pools are tiny and self-evident. e.g. you own a regional ISP and there are three logical strategic buyers. Hire an M&A attorney to negotiate.

When you almost certainly DO need a broker

  • Business value $500K – $10M. The sweet spot. Brokers reach 100× more qualified buyers than you can, and the buyer competition reliably pays for the fee with margin to spare.
  • Confidentiality is non-negotiable. If your employees, customers, or competitors finding out would damage the business, you need a broker who can market under NDA.
  • You've never sold a business. You're not negotiating a car. The deal terms (escrow, working capital adjustment, rep & warranty, earn-outs, non-competes) are where five-figure mistakes happen.
  • You can't afford to lose 6 months of focus on running the business. Selling is a part-time job. If you're already maxed out, the business will slip during the sale process — and a slipping business sells for less.

The data: brokered sales close at ~25–35% higher prices than FSBO sales of comparable businesses, and the broker fee (typically 8–12%) recovers itself with margin to spare. The exception isn't size — it's complexity.

What to ask any broker you're interviewing:

  1. How many businesses in my industry/size have you sold in the last 24 months?
  2. What's your average list-to-sale price ratio? (Should be >90%.)
  3. How do you maintain confidentiality during marketing?
  4. What's your fee structure — fixed, success, or hybrid?
  5. Who's in your buyer database, and how big is it?

If they can't answer any of those clearly, walk.
