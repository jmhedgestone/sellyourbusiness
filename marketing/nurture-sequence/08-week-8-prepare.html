If you remember nothing else from this email: preparation is the single biggest driver of sale price. Two businesses with identical SDE can sell 30% apart based on how well the seller prepared.

Here's the 12-month checklist I work through with every client. Four buckets:

📊 Bucket 1 — Financial cleanup (months 0–4)

  • Switch to accrual accounting if you're on cash basis
  • Get 3 years of CPA-reviewed financials (or audited if >$5M revenue)
  • Build an add-back tracker with documentation for each line
  • Reconcile balance sheet — every account, every month
  • Separate personal expenses cleanly (no more putting groceries through the LLC)

⚙️ Bucket 2 — Operational independence (months 2–9)

  • Document SOPs for the top 10 recurring processes
  • Cross-train at least 2 people on every critical function
  • Identify or hire a general manager / #2
  • Take a 2-week vacation in month 6 — see what breaks
  • Move the business off your personal cell, email, and bank accounts

⚖️ Bucket 3 — Legal housekeeping (months 4–10)

  • Resolve any outstanding lawsuits, employee disputes, or vendor disputes
  • Renew the commercial lease for 5–10 years (a buyer needs lease security)
  • Get all customer contracts in writing — handshake deals don't transfer
  • File any IP / trademarks formally
  • Clean up the cap table if you have minority shareholders

📈 Bucket 4 — Performance momentum (months 6–12)

  • Time the listing to your strongest TTM
  • Diversify customer concentration if needed
  • Build out recurring revenue if applicable to your industry
  • Don't take on a major one-off project that distorts the books
  • Hold price increases until after the deal closes (don't disrupt the trend buyer underwrites on)

Most owners do none of this and wonder why their business sells at 2.5× when their competitor's identical business sold at 4×. The competitor wasn't smarter. They were prepared.
