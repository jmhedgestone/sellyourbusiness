If I had to pick the single concept that separates owners who get a fair price from owners who get steamrolled at the closing table, it'd be this:

Buyers don't pay for revenue. They pay for SDE.

SDE = Seller's Discretionary Earnings. It's the total economic benefit a single working owner gets from the business. You take net income and add back:

  • The owner's salary
  • Owner perks (car, phone, travel, family on payroll if non-working)
  • Interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization
  • One-time non-recurring expenses (lawsuits, COVID losses, big tech upgrade)

That gives you a number that represents what the business actually puts in the owner's pocket, regardless of how creative the bookkeeper got with personal expenses.

Real example from a deal I closed last quarter — a Florida HVAC company:

Net income on tax return$340K
+ Owner's salary$180K
+ Personal vehicle, phone, meals$28K
+ One-time legal settlement$45K
+ D&A$72K
SDE$665K

That business sold at 3.4× SDE = $2.26M. If we'd valued it on net income alone, the owner would've left over $1M on the table.

Most owners I meet are way under-valuing themselves because they think "the business made $340K" — when in fact they take home twice that.
